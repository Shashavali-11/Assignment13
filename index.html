Taj Mahal Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, an iconic symbol of love, was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Construction began in 1632 and took over 20 years to complete, employing thousands of artisans from across the empire. The mausoleum's stunning white marble, intricate carvings, and inlaid gemstones reflect a blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.
